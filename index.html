Rulebook & Play Guide

How To Use This Guide

This guide explains the current prototype rules and uses live visual examples from the app: dice faces, card templates, board regions, pools, tile colors, and scoring examples.

The Card Library and Dice Library are still the detailed references. This rulebook is the guided play explanation.

Goal

Operate an unstable quantum reactor, shape the shared Quantum Field, and finish with the highest Quantum Potential (⬢).

The game ends when Decoherence reaches the final space. Finish the current round, then resolve final scoring.

Resources

  • Quantum Potential (⬢): victory points and the cost for Stabilizer cards.
  • Qubits (◈): resource used to play Protocol, Circuit, and State cards.
  • Instability (⧖): personal risk. Continuing usually increases it.
  • Decoherence (Δ): global timer and event track.
  • Apex (✦): title held by the player with the highest positive Collapse Value record.

Setup

  1. Choose 2 to 4 players and enter names.
  2. Each player starts with 0 qubits unless a test value is selected.
  3. Each player receives random cards in hand and face-down starting Stabilizers.
  4. Player markers begin on the Quantum Potential track in player order.
  5. Apex markers begin on the red spaces before the Apex 0 space.

Beginner Tutorial

Use this tutorial for a first learning game. Do not worry about advanced card timing yet: first learn how dice move, how Collapse Value creates a tile, and how the field gives QP.

Roll Use Actions Collapse Place Tile End Turn

1. Start With The Active Player

Look at the highlighted player tab in the top banner. That player is the active operator. Their hand, dice, qubits, instability, and card slots are shown on the right.

Beginner focus: ignore cards on the first turn unless one is glowing and you want to inspect it.

2. Press Roll

Press the arrow button between the pools. On the first press it says Roll. Qubit dice immediately add qubits, Δ dice advance Decoherence, and Ψ/outcome dice move to the Collapse Pool.

Dice PoolΦ⊗◈
Collapse PoolΨ3

3. Use Only Highlighted Actions

Action dice glow only when they have legal targets. Click a glowing action die, then click the highlighted dice in the Collapse Pool. The action moves into the Collapse Pool and creates a blue group frame.

Example: ⊗ needs two free Ψ dice. Φ needs one free Ψ die. ⊘ needs one free outcome.

4. Decide: Continue Or Collapse

If dice remain in the Dice Pool, pressing the same arrow again Continues: those dice reroll and Instability usually increases by +1⧖. If you like the Collapse Pool, press Collapse.

A safe beginner habit is: Collapse as soon as you have 2 or 3 useful dice in the Collapse Pool. Continue only when you understand what you are trying to improve.

5. Resolve The Collapse

After Collapse, every Ψ gets a random green value. Outcomes show red values. If a group shows choices above it, click one choice. When all choices are made, the app shows CV and the tile color.

Green values add. Red values subtract. Matching green values or matching red values cause Fail, which places a black tile.

6. Place The Tile

Click a legal hex. On the first turn this is around the center white hex. Later it is around your Vector. The tile color comes from CV, but QP comes from the field scoring rule.

Center Legal Legal Legal

7. Read The Field Score

Before placing, think: how many neighboring scoring tiles will this new tile touch? Green, orange, red, and violet neighbors count. White and black do not.

Beginner example: a green tile beside two scoring neighbors gives 1 + 1 + 1 = 3⬢.

8. End Turn

After placing or moving your Vector, END TURN appears. Press it when finished. Later, this is also the timing window for after-placement cards and effects.

Beginner Priorities

  • Get qubits early so you can play cards.
  • Place green and orange tiles near scoring neighbors.
  • Watch duplicate collapse values because they cause Fail.
  • Use Continue carefully; Instability is powerful but dangerous.

Common First Mistakes

  • Scoring CV directly as QP. QP usually comes from tile field scoring.
  • Trying to place anywhere after the first turn. Use your Vector neighborhood.
  • Forgetting red outcome values subtract from CV.
  • Ignoring the Decoherence events that affect all players.

Turn Structure

  1. Roll: press Roll. Qubits resolve automatically, Δ advances Decoherence, and Ψ/outcomes move to the Collapse Pool.
  2. Actions: click legal action dice in the Dice Pool and choose highlighted targets in the Collapse Pool.
  3. Continue or Collapse: Continue rerolls remaining Dice Pool dice and usually adds +1⧖. Collapse resolves the pool.
  4. Resolve Collapse: assign values, choose group results, calculate CV, check failure, and update Apex.
  5. Place Tile: choose a legal hex around the Vector and score the field effect.
  6. End Turn: play legal after-placement cards if desired, then press END TURN.

Detailed Turn Walkthrough

1. Before Roll

Check the active player banner, qubits, QP, Instability, Apex status, hand cards, and tucked cards. Circuit cards may be played before Roll when legal.

2. Roll Phase

Press Roll. Qubit faces immediately add qubits and become disabled. Delta faces advance Decoherence. Superposition and Outcome faces move to the Collapse Pool. Action faces remain in the Dice Pool.

3. Action Phase

Only dice with legal targets glow. Click an action die, then click highlighted targets in the Collapse Pool. Modifiers use one target. Relations use two or more targets. The action die moves into the Collapse Pool and forms a blue group frame.

4. Continue Or Collapse

If dice remain in the Dice Pool, the arrow becomes Continue. Continue rerolls remaining dice and usually adds +1 Instability. Collapse is available when at least one die is in the Collapse Pool.

5. Collapse Resolution

Each superposition receives a random value from 0 to 5. Outcomes show red values. For each group, choose one displayed result. Then the app calculates CV, Fail status, Annihilation pairs, Full Collapse bonus, tile color, and legal placement.

6. Tile Placement

Click one legal hex. First placement is around the center. Later placements must be around the player's Vector unless a card changes this. The tile may enter a free hex, replace a legal own tile, or conquer a legal enemy tile.

7. After Placement

The tile scores QP, applies first-placement bonuses, influences neighboring colored tiles, moves the Vector, and may trigger Stabilizers or Protocols. After-placement cards may be played before END TURN.

Game Modes

Normal: all enabled cards form one shared deck.

Flux: each player receives 5 face-down Stabilizers, then a personal 15-card deck made from 5 Protocols, 5 Circuits, and 5 States. Each player shuffles that personal deck and draws 3 starting cards. Remaining cards form the general deck.

In Flux, blue Decoherence Delta and card thresholds fire only once, then turn gray. Decoherence card thresholds draw 3 cards from each player's personal deck. Apex and card-effect draw symbols use the general deck.

Card Timing Windows

  • Before Roll: Circuits may be played to upgrade dice before rolling.
  • Action Phase: Protocols may add temporary dice, reroll dice, set values, or prepare collapse effects.
  • Before/During Collapse: some Protocols and Stabilizers modify values, groups, or dice before CV is finalized.
  • After Collapse: cards may modify CV, group values, Apex, or placement before a tile is placed.
  • After Tile Placement: cards and Stabilizers may score, move Vector, alter tile color, or trigger turn-end effects before END TURN.

Dice

  • Ψ: Superposition. During Collapse it receives a random value from 0 to 5.
  • 0-5: Outcome. Counts as a red value during Collapse.
  • ◈: Qubit. Gained automatically when rolled.
  • Δ: Decoherence. Advances the Decoherence Track unless ignored by an effect.
  • Actions: attach to free Ψ and/or Outcome dice and form groups.

2 ⊘ choose 0

Groups replace their dice values with the selected group result before total CV is calculated.

Green values add to CV. Red values subtract from CV. Group values replace the dice values inside that group.

If two or more green values match, or two or more red values match, the collapse fails. The tile becomes black.

If all six dice are in the Collapse Pool and the collapse does not fail, gain the Full Collapse bonus: +2⬢.

Apex

The first positive CV takes Apex. Later, a player takes Apex only by exceeding the current Apex value.

Positive CV moves the player's marker onto the Apex Track. Failed or negative collapses place the marker on the red spaces before 0. Passing blue ⧉ icons draws cards.

Player order is recalculated after each round from highest Apex position to lowest.

Example: placing orange beside green and red scoring neighbors gives 2 + 2 + 2 = +6⬢. White and black neighbors do not count.

QP is scored from the tile placed or reactivated, not directly from CV. Count neighboring scoring tiles only: violet, green, orange, and red. White and black neighbors do not count.

Black-2⬢ plus -2⬢ per scoring neighbor. Violet-1⬢ plus -1⬢ per scoring neighbor. White0⬢. Green+1⬢ plus +1⬢ per scoring neighbor. Orange+2⬢ plus +2⬢ per scoring neighbor. Red+3⬢ plus +3⬢ per scoring neighbor.

Replacing your own tile compares old field value to new field value and you lose the difference. Conquering compares new value to old value; the conqueror gains the difference and the defeated player resolves the old value.

Influence

After placement, the new tile influences surrounding colored tiles. Black and white tiles have no CV number and are not adjusted. Influence cannot cross zero; a tile that reaches zero becomes white.

Black-2 to neighboring colored CV. Violet-1 to neighboring colored CV. Green+1 to neighboring colored CV. Orange+2 to neighboring colored CV. Red+3 to neighboring colored CV.

Decoherence

When Δ advances onto an event space, resolve that event for all players in player order.

  • Δ swap: replace one legal action or qubit face with Δ.
  • ⧉: draw 1 card.
  • -⬢: lose the listed QP.
  • +⧖: gain 1 Instability.

Instability

  • At 1-7, if CV is negative, gain +1⧖.
  • At 8-15, if CV is positive, reduce Instability by 1.
  • At 16-25, if CV is positive, reduce Instability by 2.
  • If Instability would move past the final spot, lose 1⬢ for each extra push.

Cards

  • Protocols: require minimum QP and qubits. They give quick effects, may give Apex bonuses, and tuck as coherence symbols ⬡.
  • Circuits: played before Roll or after Collapse. They upgrade dice faces and tuck by collection symbol.
  • States: played after their condition, or Apex condition if you hold Apex, and only at legal Decoherence colors. They tuck as coherence converters.
  • Stabilizers: cost QP, enter Stabilizer slots, give permanent effects while unlocked, and lock when Instability is above their slot.

Requantize and Decohere discard a card for its listed resource effect instead of playing it.

Stabilizers

Stabilizers lock when the Instability marker is above their slot. Locked Stabilizers hide permanent and end-game text, show only the lock effect, and immediately resolve that lock effect. If Instability later moves away, the Stabilizer unlocks.

End Game Scoring

  • Presence: score the final Quantum Field by tile ownership and color.
  • Coherence: Protocol ⬡ symbols are converted by tucked State cards.
  • Resonance: Circuit symbols are converted by unlocked Stabilizers.
  • Qubits: 0-4 = 0⬢, 5-14 = +1⬢, 15-24 = +3⬢, 25-35 = +5⬢.
  • Instability: after end-game reductions, score 1-3 = +5⬢, 4-7 = +3⬢, 8-12 = +1⬢, 13-18 = 0⬢, 19-25 = -1⬢, 26 = -3⬢.
